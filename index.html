Challenge Overview

The MS-CC Environmental Science Hackathon at West Virginia State University invites students to tackle real-world sustainability and rural resilience challenges using data science, AI, and open-source tools. Students will think like cybersecurity professionals responsible for protecting real-world data systems that communities rely on.

Participants will work with data from a distributed network of low-cost air quality sensors. These networks are used in rural and under-resourced regions where traditional monitoring infrastructure is limited.

Why This Matters

These systems are vulnerable to subtle cyber threats: sensors can report faulty data, APIs can be misused, and decision systems can be misled by incomplete or manipulated information.

Your mission:
  • Investigate how failures happen
  • Design detection + prevention strategies
  • Build a sensor trust model to flag unreliable data
  • Propose practical defenses to improve resilience

Across two days, students will:

  • Learn how to access and analyze real-world air quality data using APIs and time-series workflows
  • Work in teams to investigate cyber + data integrity threats in distributed sensor networks
  • Design and test a sensor trust model to detect unreliable or compromised sources
  • Propose cybersecurity defenses to improve resilience of environmental monitoring systems
  • Present solutions with awards for technical rigor, defensive strategy, and clarity of reasoning

Top projects may be highlighted at MS-CC events and connected to future opportunities such as internships, research collaborations, and mentorship.
